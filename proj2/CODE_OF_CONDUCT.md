# Code of Conduct

This project is maintained by NCSU Team G25 and welcomes contributions from students, instructors, and other collaborators. To maintain a respectful and productive environment, all participants are expected to adhere to the following Code of Conduct.

This document is adapted from the Contributor Covenant.

---

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of different viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Use of sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information (such as physical or email addresses) without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in an academic or professional setting.

---

## Responsibilities of Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior.
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct or with the technical and academic goals of the project.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:

- Within project spaces (for example, issue trackers, pull requests, and code review).
- In public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community (for example, presenting a project talk or describing the project in academic settings).

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers of NCSU Team G25.

Reports should include:

- A description of the incident.
- When and where it occurred.
- Any relevant context and, if possible, links or screenshots.

The maintainers will review and investigate the report and will respond as appropriate to the circumstances. The maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

When addressing a violation, maintainers may take actions including but not limited to:

1. **Informal warning**  
   A private communication identifying the problematic behavior and requesting that it stop.

2. **Temporary restrictions**  
   Temporary removal of certain privileges (for example, the ability to open pull requests) for a specified period.

3. **Permanent restrictions**  
   Permanent removal from project spaces and refusal to accept further contributions in severe or repeated cases.

The specific response will depend on the severity and frequency of the violation.

---
